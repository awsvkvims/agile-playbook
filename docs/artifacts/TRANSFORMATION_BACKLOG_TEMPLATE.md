# Transformation Backlog

The transformation backlog tracks improvement initiatives required to strengthen the Agile operating model.

Unlike team backlogs, this backlog focuses on **system-level improvements**, including governance, delivery flow, engineering practices, and cultural alignment.

Audience:

- Transformation leads
- Agile coaches
- Engineering leadership
- Transformation sponsors

---

# Backlog Structure

Each backlog item should represent a **system improvement initiative**, not a feature or product change.

Example improvement areas:

- delivery flow improvements
- engineering quality improvements
- organizational alignment changes
- governance improvements
- tooling or automation improvements

# Improvement Areas

## Delivery System

Examples:

- Reduce cross-team dependencies
- Improve backlog refinement quality
- Introduce WIP limits at system level

---

## Engineering Practices

Examples:

- Increase automated test coverage
- Improve CI/CD pipeline reliability
- Reduce deployment lead time

---

## Product and Value

Examples:

- Introduce outcome-driven planning
- Improve discovery practices
- Reduce feature cycle time

---

## Culture and Leadership

Examples:

- Strengthen psychological safety
- Align leadership behaviors with Agile principles
- Improve cross-functional collaboration

---

# Backlog Review Cadence

The transformation backlog should be reviewed:

- monthly by transformation leads
- quarterly during executive transformation reviews

Items should be updated based on insights from:

- the Transformation Health Dashboard
- Maturity Assessment Reports
- Retrospectives and systemic escalations
